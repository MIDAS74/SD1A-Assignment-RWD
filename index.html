Welcome to the Clare Tourism website, your ultimate guide to exploring one of Ireland's most picturesque and captivating counties. Located on the west coast of Ireland, Clare boasts a rugged coastline, rolling hills, and charming towns and villages that are steeped in history and culture.
From the iconic Cliffs of Moher to the traditional music of Doolin and the rugged landscape of the Burren, there's something for everyone in Clare. Whether you're a nature lover, history buff, or simply looking to relax and enjoy the scenic beauty, Clare offers an unforgettable experience that will stay with you long after your visit. So sit back and let us help you plan your perfect trip to Clare.

The Burren

The Burren is a unique and stunning region of County Clare, known for its rugged karst landscape and fascinating geological features. But it's not just the natural beauty that makes the Burren so special - it's also home to charming villages like Lisdoonvarna and Ballyvaughan, as well as ancient historical sites like Poulnabrone Dolmen and Caherconnell Stone Fort.

Whether you're a nature lover, history buff, or just looking for a peaceful escape, the Burren is a must-visit destination.

Cliffs of Moher

The Cliffs of Moher are one of Ireland's most iconic natural attractions, towering 700 feet above the Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can take in breathtaking views from multiple vantage points, including O'Brien's Tower, which offers panoramic vistas of the surrounding countryside.

But the Cliffs aren't just a pretty sight - they're also home to a diverse array of wildlife, including puffins, guillemots, and dolphins. If you're looking for a quintessential Irish experience, the Cliffs of Moher are not to be missed.

Aillwee Caves

The Aillwee Caves are a fascinating underground world that's waiting to be explored. Formed by glacial meltwater over thousands of years, these limestone caves are home to impressive stalactites, stalagmites, and other rock formations.

Visitors can take a guided tour through the caverns, which includes a visit to the Birds of Prey Centre, where you can get up close and personal with some of Ireland's most majestic raptors. If you're looking for a unique and unforgettable experience, the Aillwee Caves are definitely worth a visit.

Doolin

Welcome to Doolin, a charming village on the west coast of Ireland, known for its stunning landscapes, traditional music, and warm hospitality. If you're looking for a unique and authentic Irish experience, Doolin is the perfect destination.

Come experience the sea-front face of the Cliffs of Moher, the beautiful Aran Islands, and the charming pub scene. Doolin has something for everyone.
